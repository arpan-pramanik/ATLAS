# ATLAS: Legal Notice, Ethics Policy, and Acceptable Use

## 1. Academic and Educational Purpose
The Adaptive Transport Layer Anonymization System (ATLAS) was conceived, designed, and implemented strictly as an **academic research project**. Its primary purpose is to explore the theoretical boundaries of network censorship evasion, measure the entropy of TLS morphological characteristics, and expose weaknesses in current middlebox DPI (Deep Packet Inspection) heuristics. 

This repository serves as a mathematical and practical artifact demonstrating that TLS fingerprinting (e.g., JA3/JA4) is insufficient for deterministic client identification when confronted with dynamic, adaptive entropy injection.

## 5. Dual-Use Nature and Ethical Responsibility
Tools that evade fingerprinting inherently possess a "dual-use" nature: they can protect dissidents and journalists from oppressive censorship, but they can also be abused by malicious actors to circumvent security defenses. 

We release this code open-source under the belief that security by obscurity is ineffective. By demonstrating the fragility of static TLS fingerprinting, we hope to push the industry toward more robust, behavior-based, and mathematically sound network defense paradigms.
